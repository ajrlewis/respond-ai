# Regulatory and Policy Risk Management

## Context

Alternative asset strategies are affected by changing regulation across financial services, sustainability disclosures, and sector-specific operating rules.

## Key Risk Categories

- Changes in policy support mechanisms or market design
- Evolving sustainability and disclosure obligations
- Planning, permitting, or licensing delays
- Cross-border compliance complexity where relevant

## Management Approach

Gresham House addresses regulatory risk through:

- Up-front legal and regulatory due diligence
- Conservative assumptions in underwriting
- Ongoing policy monitoring during ownership
- Governance escalation where changes could affect returns or operations

## Control Environment

Investment teams partner with legal, risk, and compliance functions to maintain current obligations registers and implement remediation plans where needed.

## Communication

Material regulatory developments and potential portfolio impacts are communicated to clients through established reporting and governance channels.
