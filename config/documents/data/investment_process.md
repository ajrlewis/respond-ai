# Investment Process

## 1. Origination

Opportunities are sourced through thematic research, long-term sector coverage, intermediary relationships, and direct sponsor/company dialogue.

## 2. Initial Screening

Each opportunity is screened for:

- Strategic fit with mandate and theme
- Risk/return profile
- Liquidity and structure considerations
- ESG and sustainability relevance

## 3. Due Diligence

Detailed due diligence typically covers:

- Commercial case and market dynamics
- Financial model and downside scenarios
- Technical and operational review
- Legal and regulatory analysis
- ESG and stewardship considerations

## 4. Investment Committee

Findings are consolidated into an investment memorandum with explicit assumptions, risk controls, and value-creation plan. Final approval is committee-led.

## 5. Execution and Structuring

Transactions are executed with documented governance rights, information rights, and risk protections appropriate for the asset and strategy.

## 6. Asset Management

Portfolio oversight includes performance tracking, covenant and compliance monitoring, ESG progress review, and periodic re-underwriting of key assumptions.

## 7. Realisation

Exit options are evaluated against market conditions and portfolio objectives, including continuation, refinancing, partial disposal, or full exit.
