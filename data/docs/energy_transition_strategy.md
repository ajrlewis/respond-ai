# Energy Transition Investment Strategy

## Overview

The Energy Transition strategy focuses on investments that support the shift to a low-carbon economy. This includes solar PV, battery storage, and related infrastructure assets.

## Investment Focus

- Ground-mounted and rooftop solar assets
- Grid-scale battery storage systems
- Co-located renewable energy and storage projects
- Supporting infrastructure (grid connections, substations)

## Geographic Scope

Primarily UK and Western Europe, with a focus on stable regulatory environments and mature renewable markets.

## Investment Criteria

- Long-term contracted or predictable cash flows
- Strong counterparties (e.g. utilities, corporates)
- Proven technologies
- Opportunities for operational optimisation

## Value Creation

Gresham House creates value through:
- Active asset management
- Optimisation of energy yield and performance
- Strategic refinancing
- Portfolio aggregation and scaling

## Risk Management

Key risks considered include:
- Power price volatility
- Regulatory changes
- Counterparty risk
- Operational performance

Mitigation strategies include:
- Diversification across assets and geographies
- Use of long-term contracts (PPAs)
- Conservative leverage structures
